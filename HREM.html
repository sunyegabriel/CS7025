About

This album, Young Easterner in Old West consists of a selection of works I have composed from the autumn of 2019 to the autumn of 2024. Each piece was fully notated, and was created independently without any formal or slight guidance.

But due to a lack of relevant musical background and resources, I have been unable to present these works in a live performance setting (except for half of one piece, which is live performed by me on a Baroque Triple Harp). As MIDI files, the compositions do not meet the necessary standards to be released on platforms. Consequently, I have had to use third-party digital platforms to modify, redesign, and export the MIDI files into a viable audio format for distribution.

To avoid copyright and originality issues concerning the built-in sounds and instruments in third-party software, and, more importantly, to achieve a historically informed sound (for example, resembling period instruments such as the Lute, Medieval Portative Organ, Cittern, Viola da Gamba, Gothic Harp, Welsh Baroque Harp, Baroque Flute (traverso, traversière), Hurdy-Gurdy, Psaltery, Crumhorn etc. which are not typically available on any software or platform), I have meticulously formulated, constructed, and simulated effects, changing all presets and made extensive post-production adjustments.

These efforts were made to approximate the authentic timbres and performance styles of the specific historical periods represented, striving to create the ideal auditory experience I envisioned. However, I apologize that many of the final works you hear may not fully capture the intended effect, due to my current limitations in the MIDI-to-audio workflow. Nonetheless, I can assure you that every detail of the final product—including instrument choices, accompaniment, harmony, and voicing—has been meticulously recorded (either written or typed) in the original scores.
